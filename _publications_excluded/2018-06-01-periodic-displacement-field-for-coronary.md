> **TL;DR:** A periodic displacement field method for nonrigid registration of coronary artery vasculature across cardiac cine sequences, exploiting the cyclic nature of heartbeat motion.

---

## What this paper is about

Tracking coronary arteries across a cardiac cine sequence is challenging because the vessels deform nonrigidly as the heart beats. Standard registration methods do not naturally account for the periodic, cyclic nature of cardiac motion, which can lead to inconsistent tracking over multiple heartbeat cycles.

## Key idea

The approach models the displacement field as a periodic function that naturally matches the cardiac cycle. By enforcing periodicity in the deformation model, the registration stays consistent across the full heartbeat, providing smooth and temporally coherent vessel tracking throughout the cine sequence.

## Why it matters

Accurate coronary artery tracking across cardiac phases is essential for motion compensation, quantitative analysis, and interventional planning. A periodic formulation provides a more physically meaningful and robust registration.
